I am currently a doctoral researcher in the Morphable Biorobotics Lab at Boston University (PI: Tommaso Ranzani). With an expected dissertation defense in Spring 2025, I am seeking a new research position where I can continue pursuing my interests after my graduation.

Research

My research predominantly focuses on stiffening strategies in wearable devices and soft robotics. Stiffening mechanisms are often used to reverse compliance and transmit force when needed in wearable and soft robotic systems. For a complete list of my scientific publications, check out my Google Scholar.

A magnetically controlled soft robotic glove for hand rehabilitation

This work presents a proof-of-concept glove that uses magnetically controlled stiffening techniques to provide targeted resistance-based rehabilitation for patients with hand motor impairments. Here's an overview video of the work.

Magnetically induced stiffening for soft robotics

This work highlights a magnetically controlled stiffening technique that combines jamming-based principles with magnetorheological fluid to implement a hybrid mechanical and materials approach to stiffness modulation.
